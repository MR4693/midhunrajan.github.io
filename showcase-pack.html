Business Context

This programme was not outsourced. Every requirement was identified through direct operational involvement — and every system was built, integrated, and deployed by the same engineer who uses the production environment every working day.

Ludwick Precision Ltd is a precision sheet metal manufacturer operating CNC fibre laser cutting, tube laser processing, CNC press brake, welding, fabrication, and assembly across a multi-work-centre shop floor. In 2025, core operational processes were paper-based: job packs were manually assembled and distributed at shift start, operators clocked in and out on paper sheets, management had no real-time view of production activity, and engineering documentation was filed manually inside MIE Trak ERP.

This document presents four ERP-integrated manufacturing systems designed and deployed from 2025 to digitise shop floor operations, automate document workflows, and deliver real-time production intelligence to management and supervisors.

From 2025, the manufacturing operation moved from a paper-based shop floor to a substantially digitised production environment — four systems live, ERP-integrated, and in active daily use across 19 operators and 14 work centres.

Manufacturing-first approach. System requirements were defined from the shop floor up — each platform solves a problem that the engineer identified through direct operational involvement across laser cutting, press brake, welding, fabrication, assembly, and production planning.

System 01 of 04

Manufacturing Operations Platform

Android-based shop floor digitisation · Live ERP integration · Brother label printing

AndroidPythonFlask SQL ServerMIE TrakREST API Brother SDKWindows Server

Business Problem

  • Paper job packs assembled and distributed manually at each shift start
  • Operator clock-in and clock-out recorded on paper sheets, entered into MIE Trak manually after the shift
  • No digital audit trail for production work — no record of who worked on what, for how long, or when
  • Move tickets handwritten; inconsistent, slow, and prone to error
  • End-of-shift data entry created daily admin overhead and ERP data lag

Objectives

  • Replace paper job packs with digital equivalents accessible on shop floor terminals
  • Enable operator clock-in and clock-out via Android, updating MIE Trak in real time
  • Establish a full digital production audit trail — operator, job, work centre, timestamps
  • Integrate Brother label printer SDK for standardised move ticket generation
  • Eliminate end-of-shift manual ERP data entry entirely

Architecture

  • Android tablets at each work centre give every operator direct access to their jobs, work instructions, and production tasks — no paper, no separate PC, no end-of-shift data entry required
  • A dedicated integration layer on the factory server connects every operator action directly to MIE Trak — the ERP updates the moment production events occur, with no manual step between the shop floor and the system
  • Live work order and job data drawn directly from the ERP database — operators see current production information, not a delayed or cached snapshot
  • Standardised move tickets printed on demand at the point of production — eliminating handwritten labels and improving shop floor part traceability
  • Deployed on the factory LAN with no external internet dependency — reliable operation within the manufacturing environment

ERP Integration

Every operator action — clock-on, clock-off, job selection, and quantity sign-off — updates the ERP at the moment it occurs. There is no end-of-shift upload, no manual data entry step, and no lag between what happens on the shop floor and what management sees in the system. MIE Trak reflects the live state of production throughout the working day, giving operations and management accurate information at all times.

Manufacturing Intelligence Platform

Real-time production dashboards · Executive and supervisor visibility · SQL-driven reporting

PythonFlaskSQL Server Jinja2MIE TrakWindows Server

Business Problem

  • Management had no real-time view of what was happening on the shop floor — production status was only known at end-of-shift, or after a physical floor walk
  • Supervisors spent time walking the production floor or calling individual operators to establish job progress — time unavailable for higher-value production management activity

Architecture

  • A dedicated dashboard server aggregates live production data and presents it to management in a browser — accessible from any device on the factory network without login or specialist knowledge
  • All data drawn live from the MIE Trak database — the same source of truth that drives production operations — ensuring management sees accurate, current production information
  • Operator data collected by the Manufacturing Operations Platform feeds directly into management dashboards — the shop floor and the management view share the same data with no duplication and no delay
  • No additional integration cost beyond the shared infrastructure already in place — the Manufacturing Operations Platform investment multiplied through shared data

System 03 of 04

Job Pack Automation System

Excel input · SQL lookup · PDF extraction · Consolidated print-ready job pack

PythonSQL ServerPDF Automation MIE TrakExcel / openpyxl

Business Problem

Job pack preparation was a time-intensive manual process: engineers or production planners would receive a list of part numbers, then manually locate the corresponding assembly PDFs across multiple customer folder locations, extract the relevant drawings, and assemble them into a single job pack for the shop floor.

This manual process was repeated multiple times per day, consumed significant engineering time, produced inconsistent results, and was a recurring source of production delays when documentation was missed, misfiled, or assembled incorrectly. Preparation time ranged from 30 minutes to several hours per batch depending on volume and document availability.

Automated Workflow

  • Engineer uploads an Excel file of part numbers — the only manual step in the entire process
  • Each part number matched automatically against the live ERP database — correct engineering documents located without any manual searching across folder structures
  • Relevant part drawings extracted from source PDFs automatically — only the required documentation included, reducing the risk of missing or incorrect pages
  • A single consolidated, correctly ordered, print-ready job pack PDF assembled and ready for shop floor distribution
  • Total preparation time: seconds per batch. No manual retrieval, no sorting, no assembly — significantly reduced risk of documentation errors

System 04 of 04

MIE Trak Document Management System

Drag-and-drop intake · Automatic part number detection · ERP filing and attachment

PythonMIE Trak APIPDF STEP FilesDXFERP Automation

Business Problem

Engineering documentation — PDFs, STEP files, DXF files — was filed manually into MIE Trak. Engineers would receive documents, identify the relevant part number, navigate to the correct customer and part folder structure, place the file, and then manually attach it to the corresponding part record in the ERP system.

This repetitive process consumed engineering time on administration rather than production-facing work, and was a persistent source of downstream problems — documents filed in the wrong location, or not attached to the correct ERP record, creating delays and errors across production, purchasing, and quality.

How It Works

  • Engineer drops documents onto the application — PDFs, STEP files, and DXF files all supported; no further manual input required
  • Part numbers identified automatically from document metadata and file naming — eliminating manual identification and the risk of human misfiling
  • Documents organised into the correct customer and part folder structure automatically — every file placed exactly where it should be in the ERP system
  • Each file attached to the corresponding part record in MIE Trak automatically — components are filed with current, searchable engineering documentation
  • Engineering document control is now a consistent, automated process — correct practice enforced regardless of who handles the files or how busy the business is
